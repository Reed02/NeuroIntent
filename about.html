About Me

I'm Cooper Le-Doux, a biomedical engineering student with a passion for brain-machine interfaces, neural decoding, and intelligent software tools for neurotechnology.

My current focus is on non-invasive EEG signal analysis and building machine learning models that extract meaningful patterns from brain data. I'm particularly interested in helping researchers and developers create accessible tools that bridge the gap between neuroscience and AI.

I'm self-taught in Python, and actively learning frameworks like MNE-Python for EEG, scikit-learn for classification, and Streamlit for creating interactive dashboards. I love building solutions that have practical value — whether it's a simple EEG visualizer or an early-stage intent decoder.

This site reflects both what I’ve built so far and where I’m headed. I believe in learning by doing, and I welcome collaboration, feedback, and mentorship.
